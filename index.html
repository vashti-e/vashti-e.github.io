About the project

SNAP, or the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, is a federal program to help supplement the food budget of needy families so they can purchase healthy food and move towards self-sufficiency. Farmers Markets are an excellent source of local, healthy food options. However, they are only a good option if they are accessible to SNAP recipients. In this project, I examine the accesiblity of Farmers Markets in Illinois and the availability of products for Illnois SNAP recipients. I used publically available data on 2019 SNAP recipients from the Illinois Department of Human Services as well as national data on Farmers Markets in the United States from Kaggle.

The number of Farmers Markets varies by county in Illinois.

In 2019, Illinois had on average 820,000 households in need of SNAP assistance each month.

Only a limited number of Farmers Markets accept SNAP and they are not necessarily easily accesible to SNAP recipients nor even to serve the number of SNAP households. For instance, only 42 of the 110 markets in Cook County (38%) accept SNAP which is not nearly enough to serve the 394,000 SNAP households in the county.

Offerings at Markets

Even when Farmers Markets accept SNAP, they may not offer all of the basic healthy food items to fulfill a balanced diet. Most markets that accept SNAP commonly have fresh fruits and vegetables available but it can be harder to find cheese, eggs, and meat.
